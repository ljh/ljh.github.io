Josh's Flow Chart

I disagree with the "what is the most important thing" approach. I think it's too simplistic.

I think it's best to go in this order:

  • What, if I don't do it at all, will have absolutely no ramifications whatsoever?
  • What, if I don't do it this week, will have no ramifications for me this semester?
  • What, if I don't do it today, will have no ramifications for me this week?

Then, when you know what you're not going to do (make the list smaller), ask:

  • What is the most critical thing for me to do this week?
  • What is the most critical thing for to prepare me for next month?
  • What is the most critical thing to prepare me for next year?

Then you sort the list and make sure you do at least one "this week" task per day, one "next month" task per week, and one "next year" task per month.

It's not a perfect system, but giving yourself permission to drop shit and leave it dropped is one of the biggest and most powerful pieces of time management that no one talks about.
